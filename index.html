The Banned Sneakers

The design of the Jordan's Air1 went against NBA's uniform matching regulations. It is rumored NBA fined Michael $5000 per game, which Nike gladly covered and used it as PR strategy.

The first pair of the Nike's Air Jordan series was designed by Peter Moore , Tinker Hathfield and Bruce Kilgore. The first model of the series was released in 1984 when Nike endorsed Michael Jordan right before his debut with the Chicago Bulls.

When Nike decided to sponsor Jordan, they could hardly imagine the impact he was going to have not only in sports but at a macro-cultural level. Jordan quickly became one of the most important references in pop culture during the 80's and the early 90's until his retirement from the NBA in 1995.

The Jordan kicks on the right represent not only a staple of culture and fashion but most importantly, they display Michael Jordan's career.
